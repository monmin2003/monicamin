Evolution of Guitars

Everything

On Christmas Day, 2012, I received my very first guitar. I had begged my parents for one for months. It was a FG1109 from First Act Discovery. That day, I sang and played 'Call Me Maybe' with my sister. I couldn't play the chords since my fingers were so small, so I put the guitar down for a few years.
3 years later, I decided to get somewhere in my music career, so I took guitar lessons. After a few lessons, I purchased my first Yamaha guitar (CG122MCH)! Although it wasn't the best-sounding guitar, it was definitely an upgrade form my first one.

After 8 months of lessons, I decided to stop. I realized that I had a better time playing guitar outside of my classes. I had to think too hard during the lessons, which I didn't enjoy and knew wasn't right. I played my Yamaha every single day, writing some of my favorite songs on it.


In 2017, I finally upgraded to a flawless Taylor 224ce-K DLX for getting good grades and graduating from middle school. It sounded absolutely beautiful and I fall in love with it every single day.

With my first two guitars, I've doodled on them, transforming them from a basic guitar, to a 'Monica Min' guitar.
